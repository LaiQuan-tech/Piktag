## The line

**PikTag — Tag yourself. Find anyone.**
Self-tag asserts who you are; the network indexes everyone you know by
what they actually do — so you find them later, by anything. New
friends, old friends, anyone you've already met.

## The insight

Everyone's network is dead weight. Your phone has 2,000 contacts and
you can't act on any of them — no context, no recall, no way to ask
"who do I know for X?". Existing tools each fix one slice and all
speak in utilitarian B2B: digital business cards (Dot/Popl/Blinq),
event lists (Luma/Partiful), relationship CRMs (Clay/Folk/Dex). None
makes the *connection itself* compound.

## What PikTag is

One loop, three moves that feed each other:

1. **Scan → exchange.** A QR scan swaps real profiles — and, for
   non-members, captures them onto the network *before* they sign up.
2. **AI tags the moment.** From place + time + context, AI auto-tags
   *how* you met and *who* they are — no typing.
3. **Tags reactivate.** Those tags become the search key that pulls a
   dormant network back to life and matches new people semantically —
   `#律師 ≈ #法律 ≈ lawyer ≈ 弁護士`, across language, automatically.

## Why it's actually different (not the 3-in-1 — the graph)

The 3-in-1 is the *window*, not the wall. Anyone funded can bolt on
the other two slices in a quarter. The wall is the **concept-linked
tag graph**: every user tags in their own words/language, the graph
unifies them by meaning, and every scan — including of *non-members* —
adds tagged, queryable signal. That graph compounds with usage and
can't be copied; competitors can copy features, not your accumulated
graph and the reactivation behavior on top of it.

## Why now

Gen-Z is post-LinkedIn-fatigue and lives in QR + camera-first UX. AI
finally makes per-moment context tagging cheap. And the incumbents
are all wearing a suit — a culturally-coded, streetwear-voiced
product can own a demographic the B2B tools won't chase.

## The wedge (cold-start is the whole game)

Growth and data are the same event. A member's scan doesn't just
trade cards — a non-member lands on a web page, sees the auto-created
meeting tags, leaves their name in one tap, and the member sees them
immediately. Every scan onboards the next user *and* enriches the
graph before that user ever installs. That is the loop we are
building to the extreme.

## Honest framing

Pre-launch. The bet isn't "we're globally unique" (true today as a
synthesis + brand, but unverifiable and temporary). The bet is: we
make the compounding loop turn before someone with distribution
notices — and then the graph, not the feature list, is the moat.
Every roadmap decision is judged against that.

## For an early user, in one breath

You'll never lose the people who matter again. Meet someone, scan,
done — PikTag remembers who, where, and why, and hands them back the
day you need them. **PikTag to connect.**
